01 — PROJECT OVERVIEW

Woven Bag Making Automation Line

At Armstrong Machinery LLP I am involved in the mechanical design and development of a woven bag making automation machine targeted at the woven PP (polypropylene) packaging industry. The machine automates the manufacturing sequence for woven bags — a product used widely in agriculture, construction, and industrial bulk packaging.

This is an industrial SPM project covering the full machine development cycle: mechanical design of all modules, detailed manufacturing drawings, BOM preparation, design reviews, coordination with manufacturing and assembly teams, pneumatic system integration, and support through installation and commissioning.

05 — ENGINEERING APPROACH

How I Work on This Project

My approach on this project is the same as on every machine I've designed — full ownership of my scope, not just the drawing production. I design with manufacture in mind, question complexity early, and maintain controlled documentation throughout so that no engineering change creates confusion on the floor.

Working in a multi-disciplinary team (Mechanical, Electrical, PLC) requires early cross-team coordination on interface points — mounting positions, cable ducts, sensor brackets, and pneumatic manifold locations. I address these at the design stage rather than discovering them during assembly.

Pneumatic system integration is a specific focus area — correct actuator sizing, valve selection, manifold layout, and circuit documentation all feed directly into machine reliability and maintenance simplicity.
