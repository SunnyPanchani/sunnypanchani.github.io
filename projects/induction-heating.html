01 — PROBLEM STATEMENT

The Engineering Challenge

Custom induction heating systems for the automotive and bearing industry demand precise heat treatment cycles, repeatable material handling, and integration with existing production lines — all within strict client budgets compared to European or Japanese imports.

Each machine in this project family required a distinct configuration tailored to the customer's component geometry, throughput requirements, and available floor space — making off-the-shelf procurement impossible.

02 — ENGINEERING APPROACH

Design Methodology

I led all mechanical design in Autodesk Inventor — covering the Exit Conveyor, Loading Automation, Coil Shuttle, Heat Station, and Power Supply Enclosure. Each sub-system was modelled as a full 3D assembly before any material was purchased, allowing interference checks, motion studies, and layout approval with the client before production.

1
Client requirements capture — translated verbal briefs into dimensioned sketches and layout proposals within first two working sessions.
2
Concept design in Inventor — full 3D models of each sub-system, reviewed and approved before any fabrication instruction issued.
3
Drawing package generation — production-ready 2D drawings, BOMs, and revision control maintained throughout.
4
Pre-dispatch verification — components checked against drawings, non-conformances resolved before shipment.
5
On-site installation and commissioning — alignment, system integration checks, parameter setting, and formal customer acceptance sign-off.

04 — DESIGN CHALLENGES

Technical Challenges Resolved

Layout integration

Fitting automated loading, coil shuttle, and heat station into constrained customer floor plans without compromising access or maintenance clearances.

Thermal management

Mechanical components near the heat station required careful material selection and thermal isolation design to maintain dimensional stability.

Production changes

Client engineering changes mid-fabrication required controlled re-issue of drawing sets while maintaining production schedule.

05 — LESSONS LEARNED

What This Project Reinforced

  • Early 3D layout approval with the client eliminates the most expensive category of engineering change — those discovered during fabrication.
  • Pre-dispatch verification is not a checkbox — it is the last practical opportunity to resolve non-conformances without site-level cost and schedule impact.
  • Drawing revision control discipline is directly proportional to the number of concurrent projects on the floor. A single un-controlled revision can cost multiple fabrication shifts.
